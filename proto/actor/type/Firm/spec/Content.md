# Firm Representing Spec

> Guide for creating a user-facing representation of a firm based on the underlying conceptual framework.

This representation operationalizes the conceptual **Firm Representation Framework** by organizing the information into three user-visible domains:

1. **Firm State**
2. **Environment State**
3. **Firm–Environment Interaction**

Each domain exposes both **current state** and **historical evolution**.

## 1. Firm State

> Representation of the firm as a social actor: its internal structure, capabilities, and organization.

### 1.1 Firm Overview

High-level description of the firm.

**Fields**

* Firm Name
* Legal Form
* Founding Year
* Headquarters Location
* Operational Regions
* Industry Classification
* Strategic Description

### 1.1 Firm Genesis

> Initial configuration of the firm at the time of formation.

**Fields**

* Founding year
* Founders
* Initial mission / strategy
* Initial market focus

**Structural Profiles**

* **Technical Profile**

  * Production technologies
  * Core technical competencies
  * R&D capabilities

* **Product Catalog Profile**

  * Initial products/services
  * Product categories
  * Target segments

* **Organizational Structure**

  * Governance structure
  * Ownership structure
  * Key organizational units

* **Capital Structure**

  * Initial capital sources
  * Debt/equity composition
  * Investors

* **Capability Profile**

  * Operational capabilities
  * Managerial capabilities
  * Technical talent base

### 1.2 Technical Profile

Description of the firm’s technological base and production systems.

**Fields**

* Core Technologies
* Production Processes
* Technical Infrastructure
* Key Technical Assets
* R&D Focus Areas
* Intellectual Property Portfolio

### 1.3 Product Catalog

Representation of what the firm produces.

**Fields**

* Product Categories
* Product Lines
* Individual Products
* Product Specifications
* Target Markets
* Product Lifecycle Stage

### 1.4 Organizational Structure

Representation of the firm’s governance and organizational architecture.

**Fields**

* Governance Structure
* Executive Leadership
* Organizational Units
* Functional Departments
* Decision Structure
* Management Hierarchy

### 1.5 Capital Structure

Representation of the firm’s financial structure.

**Fields**

* Ownership Structure
* Equity Composition
* Debt Structure
* Major Investors
* Capitalization History

### 1.6 Capability Profile

Representation of the firm’s operational capabilities.

**Fields**

* Manufacturing Capabilities
* R&D Capabilities
* Logistics Capabilities
* Marketing Capabilities
* Operational Scale
* Workforce Characteristics

## 2. Firm Evolution

> Representation of how the internal structure of the firm **changes over time**.

Each dimension of the firm includes an **evolution record**.

### 2.1 Technical Evolution

Tracks changes in the firm’s technological base.

Type of Changes:

* New technologies adopted
* Process improvements
* R&D breakthroughs
* Infrastructure upgrades

### 2.2 Product Catalog Evolution

Tracks the evolution of the firm’s offerings.

Type of changes:

* New products introduced
* Product discontinuations
* Product redesigns
* Expansion into new product categories

### 2.3 Organizational Evolution

Tracks structural changes in the organization.

Type of changes:

* Leadership changes
* Organizational restructuring
* Creation of new departments
* Strategic reorientation

### 2.4 Capital Structure Evolution

Tracks financial structural changes.

Type of changes:

* Equity issuance
* Debt restructuring
* Mergers and acquisitions
* Ownership changes

## 3. Environment State

> Representation of the environment within which the firm operates.

### 3.1 Industry Profile

Description of the industry structure.

**Fields**

* Industry Size
* Industry Growth Rate
* Major Industry Segments
* Key Competitors
* Industry Concentration

### 3.2 Market Profile

Representation of the markets served by the firm.

**Fields**

* Market Size
* Market Segments
* Geographic Markets
* Customer Types
* Demand Characteristics

### 3.3 Value Chain Structure

Representation of the production network.

**Fields**

* Upstream Suppliers
* Key Inputs
* Production Networks
* Distribution Channels
* Downstream Markets

### 3.4 Technological Landscape

Representation of technologies shaping the industry.

**Fields**

* Core Industry Technologies
* Emerging Technologies
* Innovation Trends
* Technological Disruptions

### 3.5 Regulatory Environment

Institutional environment affecting the firm.

**Fields**

* Relevant Regulations
* Licensing Requirements
* Trade Policies
* Compliance Constraints

### 3.6 Infrastructure Environment

Infrastructure supporting the firm’s operations.

**Fields**

* Transport Infrastructure
* Energy Infrastructure
* Telecommunications Infrastructure
* Industrial Clusters
* Logistics Networks

## 4. Environment Evolution

> Representation of structural changes in the environment.

### 4.1 Market Evolution

Type of changes:

* Market expansion: We need a graph by year.
* Market contraction
* Demand shifts
* New market segments

### 4.2 Technological Evolution

Type of changes:

* Technology breakthroughs
* Industry technological transitions
* Adoption of new production paradigms

### 4.3 Demand Evolution

Type of changes:

* Changing consumer preferences
* New demand drivers
* Demand volatility: We need a graph by year.

### 4.4 Regulatory Evolution

Type of changes:

* Policy changes
* Deregulation or new restrictions
* Trade policy shifts

### 4.5 Infrastructure Evolution

Type of changes:

* New transport networks
* Energy system expansion
* Logistics modernization

## 5. Firm–Environment Interaction

> Representation of outcomes generated by the interaction between the firm and its environment.

These represent **observable results of the firm’s interaction with markets and competitors.**

### 5.1 Sales Evolution

Tracks the evolution of firm sales.

Note: Every thing by year.

**Metrics**

* Revenue
* Revenue by product
* Revenue by region
* Revenue growth rate

### 5.2 Competitive Position

Tracks how the firm compares with competitors.

**Metrics**

* Relative scale
* Cost position
* Differentiation
* Innovation position

### 5.3 Market Share Evolution

Tracks the firm’s share of its target markets.

**Metrics**

* Overall market share
* Segment share
* Regional share

### 5.4 Market Coverage

Tracks the expansion or contraction of the firm’s market reach.

**Metrics**

* Geographic coverage
* Product coverage
* Customer segment coverage

### 5.5 Profitability Evolution

Tracks financial performance outcomes.

**Metrics**

* Profit margins
* Return on capital
* Operating profitability
* Profit growth
